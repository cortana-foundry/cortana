# NFL Offseason Calendar 2026: Key Dates & What to Watch
## February - September 2026

> Your roadmap to understanding the NFL year-round. Never miss a key date again.

---

## February 2026 - Post-Super Bowl

### **February 8** 🏆
**Super Bowl LX** - Levi's Stadium, Santa Clara, CA  
**Patriots defeat Seahawks** - Championship concluded  
**What to Watch:** Post-game analysis, MVP award, retirement announcements

### **February 17**
**Franchise/Transition Tag Window Opens**  
**What It Means:** Teams can designate one player each with franchise or transition tag  
**What to Watch:** Which stars get tagged, contract negotiation leverage

### **February 23 - March 2**
**NFL Scouting Combine** - Lucas Oil Stadium, Indianapolis  
**What It Means:** Top college prospects work out for NFL teams  
**What to Watch:**
- QB workouts and interviews (most important)
- 40-yard dash times and athletic testing
- Medical evaluations and character interviews
- Position-specific drills

---

## March 2026 - Free Agency Frenzy

### **March 3** ⏰
**Franchise/Transition Tag Deadline** - 4:00 PM ET  
**What to Watch:** Last-minute tag decisions, salary cap implications

### **March 9-11** 🔥
**Legal Tampering Period** - 12:00 noon ET March 9 to 3:59:59 PM ET March 11  
**What It Means:** Teams can negotiate with pending free agents but not sign them  
**What to Watch:** 
- Contract frameworks agreed to in principle
- Twitter reports and "agreements pending physical"
- Agent negotiations and leverage plays

### **March 11** 💰
**Free Agency Begins** - 4:00 PM ET  
**2026 League Year Officially Starts**  
**What It Means:** Free agents can officially sign with new teams  
**What to Watch:**
- First wave signings (biggest names usually sign quickly)
- Surprise team changes
- Salary cap casualties released by teams
- Trade deadline begins

### **March 11 - April**
**Free Agency Feeding Frenzy**  
**Phases to Watch:**
- **Day 1-3:** Tier 1 free agents (QBs, edge rushers, WRs)
- **Week 2-4:** Tier 2 players (solid starters, role players)  
- **April-July:** Bargain hunting, post-draft signings
- **July-August:** Training camp injury replacements

### **March 29 - April 1**
**Annual League Meeting** - Phoenix, Arizona  
**What It Means:** Owners vote on rule changes, discuss league business  
**What to Watch:** Rule proposals, competitive balance discussions

---

## April 2026 - Draft Preparation

### **April 6**
**Offseason Workout Programs Begin** (new head coaches)  
**What It Means:** Teams with new coaches can start voluntary workouts  
**What to Watch:** New coaching staff implementation, player buy-in

### **April 15** ⏰
**Draft Visit/Workout Deadline**  
**What It Means:** Last day for teams to bring prospects to facility  
**What to Watch:** Which players get coveted "Top 30" visits

### **April 17**
**Restricted Free Agent Offer Sheet Deadline**  
**What It Means:** Other teams must submit offers by this date  
**What to Watch:** Poison pill contracts, team matching decisions

### **April 20**
**Offseason Programs Begin** (returning head coaches)  
**What It Means:** Most teams can start voluntary workouts  
**What to Watch:** Player participation, holdout situations

### **April 22** ⏰
**Multiple Deadlines:**
- RFA right of first refusal deadline  
- Final day for any draft-eligible player contact
**What to Watch:** Teams matching RFA offers, final draft prep

### **April 23-25** 🎯
**NFL Draft** - Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania  
**Most Important NFL Event Besides Super Bowl**

#### **Round 1 (Thursday Night)**
**What to Watch:**
- QB selections and team trades up
- Surprise picks vs. consensus big board
- Team needs vs. best player available
- Trade activity and draft day deals

#### **Rounds 2-3 (Friday Night)**  
**What to Watch:**
- Day 2 steals and reaches
- Run on specific positions
- Compensatory pick trades

#### **Rounds 4-7 (Saturday)**
**What to Watch:**  
- UDFA prospects who could make rosters
- Future development picks
- Special teams specialists

**Draft Analysis Topics:**
- Immediate impact rookies vs. development projects
- Team grade analysis (A through F grades)
- Salary cap implications of rookie contracts
- Historical draft class comparisons

---

## May 2026 - Post-Draft Development

### **May 1** ⏰
**Fifth-Year Option Deadline**  
**What It Means:** Teams must decide on 2023 first-round picks  
**What to Watch:** Which players get expensive fifth year, contract negotiations

### **May 1-4 OR May 8-11**
**Rookie Minicamps** - One three-day weekend per team  
**What It Means:** First on-field work for drafted rookies  
**What to Watch:**
- QB adaptation to pro offense
- Rookie mistakes and learning curves  
- Undrafted free agent standouts
- Injury concerns

### **May 11**
**Rookie Football Development Programs Begin**  
**What It Means:** Extended rookie education and development  
**What to Watch:** Position coach installation, playbook learning

### **May 13-17**
**NFLPA Rookie Premiere** - Los Angeles  
**What It Means:** Marketing/trading card photo shoots for top rookies  
**What to Watch:** Player marketability, endorsement potential

### **May 19-20**
**Spring League Meeting** - Orlando  
**What It Means:** Owners continue league business discussions  
**What to Watch:** Competition committee reports, schedule discussions

---

## June 2026 - Team Building Phase

### **June 1** ⏰
**"June 1 Tender" Deadline** - Restricted free agents  
**What It Means:** Contract tender modifications for cap purposes  
**What to Watch:** Cap manipulation, player market values

### **June 15** ⏰
**RFA "June 15 Tender" Deadline**  
**What It Means:** Teams can reduce qualifying offers while keeping rights  
**What to Watch:** Salary cap management, player negotiations

### **Late June**
**Rookie Readiness Program** - Individual clubs  
**What It Means:** Team-specific rookie development programs  
**What to Watch:** Player adaptation, coaching staff evaluation

### **June-July**
**OTA Season** (Organized Team Activities)
**What to Watch:**
- Voluntary workout attendance (holdout watch)
- Position battles emerging
- New player integration (draft picks, free agents)
- Scheme installation progress
- Injury updates and recovery timelines

---

## July 2026 - Training Camp Approaches  

### **July 15** ⏰
**Franchise Player Contract Deadline** - 4:00 PM ET  
**What It Means:** Last day for multiyear deals with franchise players  
**What to Watch:** Tag-and-trade scenarios, holdout situations

### **Mid-July**
**Training Camps Open**  
**Schedule:**
- **Rookies:** 7 days before veterans
- **QBs/Injured Players:** 5 days before other veterans  
- **Veterans:** Mandatory reporting date

### **July 22** ⏰
**UFA Tender Signing Deadline** - 4:00 PM ET  
**What It Means:** End of signing period for certain free agents  
**What to Watch:** Last-minute veteran signings, roster construction

---

## August 2026 - Training Camp & Preseason

### **Training Camp Storylines to Follow:**

#### **Position Battles**
- Starting QB competitions
- Offensive line combinations
- Secondary depth and starters
- Special teams units

#### **Roster Bubble Watch**
- 90-man roster cuts to 53
- Practice squad eligible players  
- Veteran cuts vs. rookie development
- Special teams value players

#### **Contract Situations**
- Holdouts and "hold-ins"
- Extension negotiations during camp
- Injury settlement discussions

### **Preseason Games** (Usually 3 per team)
**Game 1 (Mid-August):** Starters play 1-2 series  
**Game 2 (Late August):** Starters play 1-2 quarters  
**Game 3 (Early September):** Dress rehearsal or rest starters

**What to Watch:**
- Rookie performance and development
- Depth chart establishment
- Special teams evaluation
- Injury concerns and management

### **Late August** ⏰
**Roster Cuts to 53 Players**  
**What to Watch:**
- Surprise cuts and releases
- Waiver wire claims
- Practice squad formations
- Trade deadline activity

## Key Recurring Themes to Follow

### **Salary Cap Management**
- **Pre-Free Agency:** Cap space creation via cuts/restructures
- **Free Agency:** Big spending vs. conservative approaches  
- **Post-Draft:** Rookie contract impact
- **Training Camp:** Extension timing and cap implications

### **Injury Management**
- **Combine:** Medical red flags
- **OTAs/Minicamp:** Soft tissue injuries, recovery timelines
- **Training Camp:** Contact injury risks, veteran rest
- **Preseason:** Starter protection vs. evaluation needs

### **Depth Chart Evolution** 
- **Free Agency:** Immediate starter additions
- **Draft:** Rookie development timelines
- **OTAs:** Position battle emergence  
- **Training Camp:** Competition resolution
- **Preseason:** Final evaluation and decisions

### **Coaching Staff Changes**
- **Hiring Cycle:** January-February coordinator moves
- **Implementation:** Scheme installation during offseason
- **Evaluation:** Training camp and preseason assessment
- **Adjustment:** Regular season adaptation
