## Basic Stats (Start Here)

### Passing Stats
| Stat | What It Measures | Good/Bad Benchmark |
|------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Completion %** | Passes completed ÷ passes attempted | 65%+ good, 70%+ elite |
| **Pass Yards** | Total passing yards gained | 250+ per game solid |
| **Pass TDs** | Touchdown passes thrown | 25+ per season good |
| **Interceptions** | Passes caught by defense | <10 per season ideal |
| **Passer Rating** | NFL's official QB efficiency formula | 100+ good, 110+ elite |

### Rushing Stats
| Stat | What It Measures | Good/Bad Benchmark |
|------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Rush Yards** | Total yards gained running | 4.0+ YPC good, 5.0+ elite |
| **YPC (Yards Per Carry)** | Average yards per rushing attempt | 100+ yards/game solid |
| **Rush TDs** | Rushing touchdowns | 10+ per season good |
| **Attempts** | Number of rushing attempts | Volume indicator |

### Receiving Stats  
| Stat | What It Measures | Good/Bad Benchmark |
|------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Receptions** | Catches made | 80+ good, 100+ elite |
| **Rec Yards** | Total receiving yards | 1000+ good, 1300+ elite |
| **Rec TDs** | Receiving touchdowns | 8+ good, 12+ elite |
| **YAC** | Yards After Catch | Shows elusiveness |
| **Targets** | Passes thrown to player | Volume indicator |

### Defense Stats
| Stat | What It Measures | Good/Bad Benchmark |
|------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Tackles** | Defensive stops made | 100+ good for LB/S |
| **Sacks** | QB tackles behind line | 10+ good, 15+ elite |
| **Interceptions** | Passes caught by defense | 3+ good for CB/S |
| **PBUs** | Pass Break-Ups/Pass Defenses | 10+ good for CB |
| **Forced Fumbles** | Fumbles caused by defender | Ball disruption metric |

## Intermediate Stats (Level Up)

### Efficiency Metrics
| Stat | What It Measures | Why It Matters |
|------|------------------|----------------|
| **Y/A** | Yards per attempt (passing) | QB efficiency beyond completion % |
| **AY/A** | Adjusted yards per attempt | Factors in TDs and INTs |
| **ANY/A** | Adjusted net yards per attempt | Includes sacks (most complete basic QB stat) |
| **Target Share** | % of team's targets to one receiver | Shows offensive role/usage |
| **Air Yards** | Distance ball travels in air | Separates QB arm from YAC |
| **Red Zone %** | TD% when inside 20-yard line | Finishing ability in prime scoring area |

### Situational Stats
| Stat | What It Measures | Context |
|------|------------------|---------|
| **3rd Down %** | Success rate on 3rd down | Crucial for sustaining drives |
| **4th Down %** | Success rate on 4th down | Aggressive coaching/execution |
| **Turnover Margin** | Turnovers gained - turnovers lost | Strong predictor of wins |
| **Pressure Rate** | % of dropbacks with QB pressure | Pass rush effectiveness |
| **Sack Rate** | Sacks per dropback | QB protection quality |

### Team Efficiency
| Stat | What It Measures | What Good Looks Like |
|------|------------------|----------------------|
| **Points Per Drive** | Average points scored per possession | 2.0+ good, 2.5+ elite |
| **Plays Per Drive** | Drive sustainability | 5.5+ plays indicates good offense |
| **Time of Possession** | Clock control | 31+ minutes shows control |
| **Penalty Yards** | Discipline/coaching | <60 yards/game good |

## Advanced Analytics (Pro Level)

### EPA (Expected Points Added)
**What It Is:** Measures how much a play increases/decreases expected points  
**Why It Matters:** Accounts for down, distance, and field position context  
**How to Read:** Positive = good, negative = bad. +0.1 EPA per play is solid  
**Example:** 3rd & 10 completion for 12 yards = high EPA (converts first down)

**EPA Variations:**
- **EPA per play** - Overall efficiency 
- **EPA per dropback** - Passing efficiency
- **EPA per rush** - Rushing efficiency  
- **Target EPA** - Individual receiver value

### DVOA (Defense-Adjusted Value Over Average)
**What It Is:** Football Outsiders' efficiency metric adjusted for opponent strength  
**Why It Matters:** Accounts for strength of schedule that basic stats miss  
**How to Read:** % above/below average. +20% = elite, -20% = terrible  
**Example:** Patriots offense might have good yards but face weak defenses

**DVOA Categories:**
- **Offensive DVOA** - Offense vs. average, adjusted for opponent D
- **Defensive DVOA** - Defense vs. average, adjusted for opponent O  
- **Total DVOA** - Overall team efficiency
- **Weighted DVOA** - Emphasizes recent games

### PFF Grades (Pro Football Focus)
**What It Is:** 0-100 grades based on film review of every play  
**Why It Matters:** Measures execution independent of results/scheme  
**How to Read:** 90+ = elite, 80-89 = good, 60-79 = average, <60 = poor  
**Example:** RB who breaks tackles gets higher grade than one who relies on blocking

**PFF Subscores:**
- **Overall Grade** - Weighted average of all snaps
- **Pass/Run Grades** - Situation-specific performance
- **Pass Rush/Coverage** - Defensive specialist grades

### QBR (ESPN Total Quarterback Rating)
**What It Is:** ESPN's proprietary QB metric (0-100 scale)  
**Why It Matters:** Adjusts for game situation, opponent, teammates  
**How to Read:** 75+ = elite, 65+ = good, 50+ = average  
**Difference from Passer Rating:** Includes rushing, context, clutch situations

### Win Rate Stats (NFL Next Gen)
**Pass Rush Win Rate:** % of rushes that beat blocker within 2.5 seconds  
**Pass Block Win Rate:** % of blocks that protect QB for 2.5+ seconds  
**Run Stop Win Rate:** % of run plays where defender beats block  
**Open Score:** WR separation metric based on GPS tracking

## Next-Level Analytics (Expert)

### Expected Points Model
**Concept:** Every situation (down, distance, field position) has historical point value  
**Applications:** EPA, Go-for-it decisions, game management  
**Example:** 1st & 10 at opponent 25 = ~4.2 expected points

### Win Probability  
**What It Is:** % chance team wins given current game state  
**Factors:** Score, time remaining, field position, down/distance  
**Applications:** Clutch performance measurement, coaching decisions  
**Example:** Down 3 with 2 minutes left, ball at own 20 = ~35% win probability

### Air Yards vs YAC
**Air Yards:** Distance ball travels in air to receiver  
**YAC (Yards After Catch):** Yards gained after reception  
**Why Split Matters:** Separates QB accuracy from receiver elusiveness  
**Advanced Metrics:**
- **CPOE:** Completion % Over Expected (based on air yards, coverage)
- **YAC Over Expected:** Receiver elusiveness beyond average
- **ADOT:** Average Depth of Target (QB aggressiveness)

### Pressure Statistics
**Pressure Rate:** % of dropbacks with pass rush pressure  
**Time to Pressure:** How quickly pressure arrives  
**Blitz Rate:** % of plays defense sends extra rusher  
**Clean Pocket Rate:** % of dropbacks without pressure

### Coverage Metrics
**Target Separation:** Distance between receiver and nearest defender  
**Coverage Type:** Man, zone, press, off-coverage breakdowns  
**Expected Completion %:** Based on distance, coverage, pass location  
**Contested Catch Rate:** Success on tight-window throws

## How to Use Stats Like a Pro

### 1. Context is King
- **Sample Size:** 3 games ≠ season trend
- **Opponents:** Stats vs. good teams matter more
- **Game Script:** Garbage time skews numbers
- **Weather/Venue:** Factor in conditions

### 2. Combine Multiple Metrics
- Don't rely on one stat
- Use basic + advanced together  
- Compare efficiency vs. volume
- Consider both offense and defense

### 3. Understand Limitations
- **EPA:** Great for aggregate, noisy for single plays
- **PFF Grades:** Subjective, don't see play calls
- **Basic Stats:** Miss context and efficiency
- **Team Stats:** Influenced by schedule strength

### 4. Look for Trends
- **Hot/Cold streaks:** Last 4-6 games vs. season
- **Home vs. Road splits:** Travel/comfort factors  
- **Division games:** Different intensity/familiarity
- **Prime time:** Pressure performance

### 5. Player Development Arcs
- **Rookie seasons:** Steep learning curves
- **Year 2-3:** Typical breakout windows
- **Veterans:** Decline curves, injury impact
- **Peak ages:** QB (27-32), RB (22-26), etc.

## Stat Combinations That Tell Stories

### Elite QB Indicators
- **High EPA + High CPOE + Low Sack Rate** = Complete QB
- **High Air Yards + High ANY/A** = Aggressive downfield passer  
- **High QBR + High clutch EPA** = True game manager

### WR Quality Assessment  
- **High Target Share + High YAC Over Expected** = Elite talent
- **High Air Yards + High ADOT** = Deep threat specialist
- **High Red Zone Targets + High TD Rate** = Red zone weapon

### Defense Evaluation
- **Low EPA Allowed + High Pressure Rate** = Elite pass defense
- **Low YAC Allowed + High PBU Rate** = Sticky coverage  
- **High Stuff Rate + Low YPC Allowed** = Run-stopping defense

### Team Building Success
- **High Offensive DVOA + Low Defensive DVOA** = Championship contender
- **Positive TO Margin + High 3rd Down %** = Sustainable success
- **Low Penalty Rate + High EPA/play** = Well-coached team

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Only looking at total yards/TDs**  
✅ **Consider efficiency metrics (EPA, DVOA, ANY/A)**

❌ **Ignoring sample size**  
✅ **Weight recent performance but consider full season**

❌ **Comparing raw numbers across eras**  
✅ **Use era-adjusted metrics or relative rankings**

❌ **Dismissing context**  
✅ **Factor in opponents, game situation, venue**

❌ **Relying on one "magic" stat**  
✅ **Combine multiple metrics for complete picture**
