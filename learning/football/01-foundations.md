## Basic Rules

### The Downs System
**What are downs?** A team has **4 attempts (downs)** to advance the ball **10 yards** or more.
- **1st down**: Fresh set of 4 attempts after gaining 10+ yards
- **2nd down**: Second attempt if you didn't get 10 yards on 1st
- **3rd down**: Third attempt - often the most crucial!
- **4th down**: Final attempt - teams usually punt or kick a field goal

**What happens if you don't get 10 yards in 4 downs?**
The other team gets the ball at that exact spot (turnover on downs).

### Scoring Methods

| Method | Points | How It Works |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| **Touchdown** | 6 | Get the ball into the end zone |
| **Extra Point** | 1 | Kick through goalposts after touchdown |
| **Two-Point Conversion** | 2 | Run/pass into end zone after touchdown (instead of extra point) |
| **Field Goal** | 3 | Kick ball through goalposts during regular play |
| **Safety** | 2 | Tackle opposing player in their own end zone |

### Clock Management
- **Game Clock**: Counts down during play
- **Play Clock**: 40 seconds between plays (or 25 after certain situations)
- **Clock Stops For**: 
  - Incomplete passes
  - Out of bounds
  - Timeouts
  - Penalties
  - First downs (briefly)
  - Two-minute warning

### Common Penalties

| Penalty | Yards | Description |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| **False Start** | 5 | Offensive player moves before snap |
| **Holding** | 10 | Illegally grabbing/restraining player |
| **Pass Interference** | 15 (or spot) | Illegally preventing receiver from catching ball |
| **Offside** | 5 | Player crosses line of scrimmage before snap |
| **Unsportsmanlike Conduct** | 15 | Inappropriate behavior/celebration |
| **Roughing the Passer** | 15 | Hitting QB after ball is thrown |

## The Field Layout

```
    [END ZONE]  10  20  30  40  50  40  30  20  10  [END ZONE]
<-- 120 YARDS TOTAL -->
<-- 100 YARDS OF PLAYING FIELD -->
```

### Key Field Elements
- **Total Length**: 120 yards (including both 10-yard end zones)
- **Width**: 53⅓ yards (160 feet)
- **End Zones**: 10 yards deep at each end
- **Goal Posts**: At back of each end zone, 10 feet high, 18 feet 6 inches wide
- **Hash Marks**: Lines that mark where plays start (ball placed on nearest hash mark)
- **Yard Lines**: Every 5 yards marked, numbered every 10 yards

### The 50-Yard Line
- **Midfield**: The center of the field
- **"Red Zone"**: Area within 20 yards of the goal line (scoring is more likely)

## Game Structure

### Quarters and Time
- **4 Quarters**: 15 minutes each = 60 minutes total
- **Halftime**: 12 minutes between 2nd and 3rd quarters
- **Two-Minute Warning**: Automatic timeout with 2 minutes left in each half

### Overtime (Regular Season)
- **10 minutes maximum**
- **Sudden death** if touchdown scored
- **Field goals**: Other team gets a chance to match/exceed
- **Tie**: Game ends in tie if no one scores

### Overtime (Playoffs)
- **15-minute periods** until someone wins
- **No ties allowed** - keep playing until there's a winner

### Coin Toss
- Winner chooses to receive ball OR which goal to defend
- Loser gets the other choice
- Second half: Team that lost coin toss gets to choose

## Player Positions

### OFFENSE (11 players on field)

#### **Quarterback (QB)** - The Field General
- **Role**: Throws passes, hands off ball, calls plays
- **Key Skills**: Accuracy, decision-making, leadership
- **Famous Players**: Tom Brady, Aaron Rodgers, Patrick Mahomes

#### **Running Back (RB)** - The Ground Game
- **Role**: Carries the ball, catches short passes, blocks
- **Types**: Halfback (HB), Fullback (FB - bigger, more blocking)
- **Key Skills**: Speed, vision, ball security
- **Famous Players**: Derrick Henry, Christian McCaffrey, Saquon Barkley

#### **Wide Receiver (WR)** - The Speed Demons
- **Role**: Catch passes, run precise routes
- **Types**: Split End, Flanker, Slot Receiver
- **Key Skills**: Speed, hands, route-running
- **Famous Players**: Tyreek Hill, Davante Adams, Cooper Kupp

#### **Tight End (TE)** - The Hybrid
- **Role**: Block like lineman, catch like receiver
- **Key Skills**: Size, hands, blocking ability
- **Famous Players**: Travis Kelce, Rob Gronkowski, Mark Andrews

#### **Offensive Line (OL)** - The Protectors (5 players)
- **Center (C)**: Snaps ball to QB, calls protection
- **Guards (LG/RG)**: Block on either side of center
- **Tackles (LT/RT)**: Block on outside, protect QB's "blind side"
- **Key Skills**: Size, strength, technique

### DEFENSE (11 players on field)

#### **Defensive Line (DL)** - The Pass Rushers
- **Defensive Ends (DE)**: Rush passer, stop outside runs (2 players)
- **Defensive Tackles (DT)**: Stop inside runs, rush passer (1-2 players)
- **Nose Tackle (NT)**: Lines up over center in 3-4 defense
- **Key Skills**: Size, strength, speed
- **Famous Players**: Aaron Donald, T.J. Watt, Myles Garrett

#### **Linebackers (LB)** - The Athletes
- **Middle/Inside Linebacker**: Stop runs, cover short passes
- **Outside Linebacker**: Rush passer, cover passes, stop runs
- **Key Skills**: Speed, tackling, coverage ability
- **Famous Players**: Bobby Wagner, Micah Parsons, Roquan Smith

#### **Defensive Backs (DB)** - The Coverage Specialists
- **Cornerback (CB)**: Cover wide receivers (2 players)
- **Safety (S)**: Last line of defense
  - **Free Safety (FS)**: Deep coverage, ball hawk
  - **Strong Safety (SS)**: Run support, tight end coverage
- **Key Skills**: Speed, coverage, ball skills
- **Famous Players**: Jalen Ramsey, Stephon Gilmore, Derwin James

### SPECIAL TEAMS

#### **Kicker (K)**
- **Role**: Extra points, field goals
- **Range**: Most accurate from under 50 yards

#### **Punter (P)**
- **Role**: Kicks ball to other team on 4th down
- **Goal**: Pin other team deep in their territory

#### **Long Snapper (LS)**
- **Role**: Snaps ball for punts and field goals

#### **Return Specialists**
- **Punt Returner**: Catches punts, tries to advance
- **Kick Returner**: Returns kickoffs

## Essential Terminology

### Game Situations
1. **Snap**: Start of each play when center gives ball to QB
2. **Huddle**: Team meeting before each play to call the play
3. **Audible**: QB changing the play at the line of scrimmage
4. **Blitz**: Extra pass rushers (more than 4)
5. **Red Zone**: Area within 20 yards of goal line
6. **Two-Minute Drill**: Hurry-up offense with time running out

### Offensive Terms
7. **Handoff**: QB gives ball to running back
8. **Pitch/Toss**: QB throws ball underhand to running back
9. **Screen Pass**: Short pass behind blockers
10. **Play Action**: Fake handoff before throwing pass
11. **Bootleg**: QB rolls out to one side
12. **Scramble**: QB runs around to avoid pressure

### Defensive Terms
13. **Sack**: Tackling QB behind line of scrimmage
14. **Interception (INT)**: Defense catches a pass intended for offense
15. **Fumble**: Player loses control of the ball
16. **Strip**: Forcing a fumble by knocking ball loose
17. **Pick-Six**: Interception returned for touchdown
18. **Three-and-Out**: Forcing offense to punt after 3 plays

### Penalty Terms
19. **False Start**: Offensive player moves before snap
20. **Encroachment**: Defensive player crosses line before snap
21. **Holding**: Illegally grabbing a player
22. **Clipping**: Illegal block from behind
23. **Face Mask**: Grabbing opponent's face mask
24. **Roughing**: Illegal hit on kicker or passer

### Field Position
25. **Hash Marks**: Lines where ball is spotted for next play
26. **Sideline**: Boundary lines on sides of field
27. **Goal Line**: Line marking entrance to end zone
28. **Line of Scrimmage**: Where each play begins

### Game Management
29. **Timeout**: Stop the clock (each team gets 3 per half)
30. **Challenge**: Coach questions referee's call (2 per game)
31. **Two-Minute Warning**: Automatic timeout with 2:00 left in each half
32. **Onside Kick**: Short kickoff attempting to retain possession
33. **Fair Catch**: Signal by returner to not advance punt (can't be tackled)
34. **Coffin Corner**: Punt aimed at corner of field to pin opponent
35. **Hail Mary**: Long desperation pass, usually at end of half/game
