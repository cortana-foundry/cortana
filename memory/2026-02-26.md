Summary
- Jobs with hard "output NOTHING" rules fall into two buckets:
  1) Internal maintenance/proprioception/immune jobs (no user delivery) — explicitly supported by "self-improvement runs silently" and "Pre-filter status reports" preferences.
  2) Event-triggered alerts (calendar reminders, real-time newsletter alerts, auth checks) — silence represents "no event" and aligns with Hamel's desire to avoid noise.
- The only clearly problematic case — CANSLIM scans being suppressed when all candidates were NO_BUY — has already been corrected: the cron now always forwards the scanner's output, and MEMORY.md explicitly requires this.

Conclusion
- No additional cron prompts in config/cron/jobs.json require modification to "always forward" results at this time.
- All remaining filters are either:
  - Backed by explicit preferences (status/immune/proprioception noise suppression, CANSLIM always-forward), or
  - Intrinsic to event-triggered alert semantics where silence = "nothing happened" rather than hidden results.
